Traditional Games in Kenya

koi/kibe game

Description of a game

koi/kibe:is one of the most popular children’s game among the Swahili people of the Kenyan coast. It is a “hide and seek” game for children for children between 5 and 12 yrs.

Rukongoro game

description of a game

Rukongoro-is a traditional Ameru board game but is also common among the Tigania, Imenti, and Igembe of Mt.Kenya region

Ajwa game

Description of the game

commonly known as board game is a men only game among the Luo. The game is played on a board bearing sixteen holes; eight each side

Instructions of the games

  • koi/kibe game are played:Around ten children gather at the village playground and one is chosen to be the explorer. He/she then shuts his/her eyes and counts out the numbers 1-10 loudly. By the time he/she reaches count ten and opens the eyes, everyone is supposed to have found a place to hide. The explorer is then supposed to start seeking them out. The kid who hides longest without being discovered is awarded a prize commonly referred to as “gold medal” and the game continues with the one who was discovered first becoming the next explorer.
  • Rukongoro game It takes two to tango in this game that do not a mass from your side. The other rule is that do not use bad or foul language against opponent.
  • Ajwa game the game is usually played by two people at a time in which the winner gets a price in the form of livestock. At the onset, each player organizes his side of the board as he sees fits.Every time a player wins some chips, he puts them aside until it is no longer possible to play
